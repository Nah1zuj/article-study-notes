# Review Article Patterns

Use this reference when the material is a review, survey, state-of-the-art article, literature review, handbook chapter, encyclopedia article, or field overview.

## Do Not Summarize Source by Source

A good review note should not become a list of "A said, B said, C said." It should explain how the article organizes a field.

Ask:

- What problem requires a review?
- What field, debate, or tradition is being mapped?
- What organizing principle does the author use?
- What does the review claim is known?
- What remains unsettled?
- What research agenda or conceptual framework emerges?

## Review Type Detection

Systematic review:

- narrow research question
- explicit search strategy
- inclusion/exclusion criteria
- evidence synthesis
- often empirical or quantitative

Semi-systematic or narrative review:

- broader topic
- multiple traditions or disciplines
- thematic or historical mapping
- useful for complex areas where full systematic coverage is impractical

Integrative or critical review:

- critiques and synthesizes literature
- aims to generate a framework, model, taxonomy, or theoretical reconceptualization
- requires transparency about selection and reasoning

Theory review:

- compares, evaluates, or develops theories
- contribution lies in conceptual clarification or theory building

Research history review:

- tracks development over time
- should identify turning points, debates, and shifts, not just chronology

## Organization Patterns

Common structures:

- thematic
- chronological
- methodological
- theoretical
- school/tradition based
- debate based
- application-scenario based

Prefer thematic or theoretical organization when possible. Chronology is useful only when development over time explains the current debate.

## Contribution Types

Identify the contribution:

- state of knowledge
- concept clarification
- theme synthesis
- historical overview
- taxonomy or classification
- theoretical model or framework
- research agenda
- critique of a field

## Philosophy Review Handling

For philosophy reviews and encyclopedia-style materials:

- map the problem space
- define major positions
- reconstruct argument families
- identify central objections
- distinguish primary literature from secondary interpretation
- avoid a straight historical list unless the text itself is about historical development
